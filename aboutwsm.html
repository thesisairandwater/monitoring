Wireless Sensor Network for Air & Water Monitoring

Using various sensors, we can monitor the quality of air and water in real-time.

A Wireless Sensor Network (WSN) consists of distributed autonomous sensors that collect and transmit environmental data. These networks help in remote monitoring, enabling real-time data analysis and response.

WSNs are widely used in environmental monitoring, industrial automation, and smart cities. By deploying multiple sensors, these networks provide crucial insights into air pollution levels, water contamination, and climate conditions.

The sensors in a WSN communicate wirelessly, reducing infrastructure costs and making deployment flexible. They rely on low-power communication protocols to ensure efficient data transmission while conserving energy.

One of the key advantages of WSNs is their scalability. Additional sensors can be easily integrated to expand the monitoring area, making these networks adaptable for various applications.

Advancements in WSN technology continue to enhance their capabilities. With the integration of machine learning and IoT, these networks are becoming smarter, enabling predictive analytics and automated decision-making for better environmental management.
