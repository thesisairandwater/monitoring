pH Sensor

Description

A pH sensor measures the acidity or alkalinity of a solution by detecting the hydrogen ion activity in the solution. It is commonly used in water quality monitoring, aquariums, agriculture, and various industrial applications.

This sensor provides precise readings, making it an essential tool for maintaining optimal pH levels in critical environments.

What is pH?

pH: A scale used to specify the acidity or basicity of an aqueous solution, pH ranges from 0 to 14. A pH below 7 indicates acidity, while a pH above 7 indicates alkalinity. A pH of 7 is considered neutral. Monitoring pH is vital in numerous fields, such as agriculture, water treatment, and healthcare.

Fun Facts

  • Precision: Measures pH values from 0 to 14 with high accuracy.
  • Applications: Widely used in environmental monitoring, aquaculture, and food safety.
  • Durability: Designed to withstand harsh environments for extended periods.
  • Compact: Easy to integrate into portable and IoT devices.
  • Calibration: Requires periodic calibration for consistent accuracy.

Additional Information

  • Power Requirements: Operates on 3.3V or 5V, making it compatible with most microcontrollers.
  • Output: Provides analog voltage output proportional to the pH level.
  • Temperature Range: Functions effectively between 0°C and 50°C.
  • Maintenance: Regular cleaning and proper storage extend the electrode's lifespan.
  • Compact Design: Lightweight and portable for field use.
