PMS5003 Sensor

Description

The PMS5003 is a highly accurate laser-based particulate matter sensor that measures PM1.0, PM2.5, and PM10 concentrations in real time. It uses advanced light scattering technology to detect fine particles as small as 0.3 microns, providing crucial air quality data.

With its compact design and simple communication protocol, it’s perfect for environmental monitoring, IoT devices, and smart home applications. The sensor provides consistent and reliable readings, making it a favorite among DIY enthusiasts and professionals alike.

What Are PM2.5 and PM10?

PM2.5: These are fine particles with a diameter of 2.5 microns or smaller, typically produced by combustion processes like vehicle emissions, thermal power plants, and forest fires. Due to their small size, they can penetrate deep into the lungs and bloodstream, potentially causing health issues such as asthma, cardiovascular disease, and respiratory infections. The 24-hour average PM2.5 limit is set at 35 µg/m³.

PM10: These larger particles, up to 10 microns in diameter, often come from activities like stone crushing, coal grinding, road dust stirred by vehicles, and industrial processes. PM10 can irritate the respiratory system and exacerbate existing lung conditions. The 24-hour average PM10 limit is 150 µg/m³.
